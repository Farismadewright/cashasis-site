**Behind on Wayne County property taxes does not mean you have already lost your house.** You still have time to sell, clear the debt, and walk away with whatever is left over instead of letting the county take it. The key is acting before the March 31 deadline, not after.

## How tax foreclosure works in Wayne County

Michigan moves faster than most states. Under the 1999 law, the time to pay back taxes was cut from about five years to roughly two. Here is the basic clock:

1. Your taxes go delinquent and interest and fees start adding up each month.
2. The property enters forfeited status, and the costs climb higher.
3. If the taxes are still unpaid by **March 31 in the third year of delinquency**, the property is foreclosed.
4. On **April 1**, the title transfers to the Wayne County Treasurer. At that point it is no longer yours to sell.

That March 31 date is the line in the sand. Once it passes, your options shrink to almost nothing.

## Why a sale clears the problem cleanly

When you sell, the back taxes get paid directly out of the closing. You do not need to come up with the money first. The title company handles the payoff, the county gets made whole, and the debt comes off your name.

For a house buried in taxes, that is often the cleanest exit. No payment plan to keep up with for years, no interest stacking every month, no deadline hanging over you. You sell, the debt clears at closing, and you move on.

## Know about payment plans and surplus money too

Selling is not the only path, and we will tell you straight if another option fits you better.

- The **Wayne County Treasurer** offers taxpayer assistance payment plans. If you can stay current going forward, a plan can keep you in the home.
- **Detroit's HOPE program** (Homeowners Property Exemption) can exempt eligible homeowners from current-year taxes based on household income.
- If a property does get foreclosed and sold at auction, Michigan law now requires the county to pay back **surplus proceeds** once your tax debt and fees are covered. Former Wayne County owners have already been paid back millions. But there are filing deadlines, and it is always better to sell on your own terms before it gets to that point.

You can look up your property's status directly on the Wayne County Treasurer's site.
