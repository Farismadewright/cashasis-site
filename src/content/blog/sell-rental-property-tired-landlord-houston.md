If you're done being a landlord, here's the relief: **you can sell your Houston rental fast for cash — occupied or vacant, problem tenants or not, any condition — without evicting anyone or fixing a thing.** A cash buyer who works with investors takes it as-is, lease and all.

## You bought an investment, not a second job

It made sense at first. But the midnight calls, the turnover, the repair bills, the late rent, the eviction hearings — it adds up. At some point the property stops being passive income and starts being a part-time job you didn't sign up for.

The traditional way out is painful: you'd typically need the unit vacant, cleaned up, and repaired before listing — which means ending tenancies, eating vacancy costs, and pouring money into a place you're trying to leave.

## Selling as-is skips all of it

A cash sale to an investor buyer flips the whole thing:

- **Sell it occupied.** The buyer takes the property with tenants and the lease in place. No need to empty it first.
- **No eviction required.** Got problem tenants? That's often the exact reason to sell this way — you hand over the situation, the buyer deals with it.
- **No repairs.** Deferred maintenance, tenant damage, dated everything — priced in, not fixed by you.
- **No agent, no showings.** No coordinating access around tenants who don't want strangers walking through.

## The numbers a tired landlord should actually run

Sellers assume a cash offer means leaving money on the table. Run the real comparison. A traditional sale of a rental costs you:

- **Agent commissions** on the sale.
- **Repairs and turnover** to make it listable.
- **Vacancy** while it sits empty and listed (no rent coming in, taxes and insurance still due).
- **Possible eviction costs** if you're clearing tenants first.

A clean cash sale removes all of that in one move. What looks like a lower sticker number is often more money in your pocket — and far less aggravation.
